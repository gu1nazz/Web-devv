Recipe #1

Spicy salmon rice bowls by Rachel Phipps Transform salmon fillets into these spicy gochujang salmon bite bowls in no time. The perfect healthy lunch or dinner. Each serving provides 327 kcal, 19.9g protein, 34.4g carbohydrate (of which 2.4g sugars), 11.8g fat (of which 2.1g saturates), 1.4g fibre and 1.3g salt.

  • Time: 40 mins
  • Serves: 1
Ingredients Quantity
gochujang 2 tbsp
rice vinegar 1 tbsp
soy sauce 1 tsp
clove 1 small garlic,crushed
minced fresh root ginger 1 tsp
salmon fillets, skin removed and cut into 2cm/¾in cubes 2 large
pouches microwave sticky rice 2 x 250g
vegetable oil ½ tbsp
cucumber, peeled and chopped into 1cm cubes 1/3
kimchi 100g/3½oz

Method

Step 1: Whisk together the gochujang, rice vinegar, soy sauce, garlic and ginger in a shallow dish to make a smooth paste.

Step 2:Add the salmon to the gochujang mixture and stir to combine.

Step 3:Heat the rice according to the packet instructions and set aside.

Step 4:Heat the oil in a non-stick frying pan over a high heat. Once the oil is shimmering, add the salmon pieces. Almost as soon as you’ve added the salmon to the pan, turn the bites over with tongs. Add the rest of the sauce from the dish and stir before removing from the heat. The salmon bites should be just cooked through.

Step 5:Serve the salmon and any sauce from the pan with the rice, cucumber and kimchi. Sprinkle with the toasted sesame seeds, if using.

Recipe #2

Linguine alla puttanesca By Anna Del Conte. Puttanesca is a fiery, easy sauce for pasta that can be made in less time than it takes to cook a pan of linguine. Headshot of Anna Del Conte

  • Time: 40 mins
  • Serves: 2
Ingredients Quantity
dried linguine 200g/7oz
extra virgin olive oil 2 tbsp
tin chopped tomatoes 200g
garlic clove 1
salted anchovies 2
dried chilli flakes 2 pinches
capers, rinsed 1 tbsp
black olives 12

Method

Step 1: Add 2 tablespoons salt to 2 litres water and bring to the boil in a large saucepan. Add the pasta and return to the boil quickly. Cook until al dente.

Step 2:Meanwhile, heat the oil in a frying pan. Add the tomatoes and a pinch of salt and cook over a high heat for 2–3 minutes, stirring often.

Step 3:Roughly chop the garlic and anchovies with the chilli flakes and add them to the tomatoes, turn the heat down to low and cook for 2–3 minutes, mashing the anchovies into a paste using a wooden spoon or fork.

Step 4:Stir in the capers and olives and cook for another minute. Meanwhile, quickly drain the pasta, then add it to the sauce and heat through.

Step 5:Divide the pasta puttanesca between two plates and tear over the basil leaves before serving.

Recipe #3

Salmon Paste By Rosie Reynolds. For such a simple dish, this salmon pasta tastes really special. Slowly cooking the leeks and spring onions brings out their sweetness which complements the salmon so well. A good recipe for date night! Each serving provides 669 kcal, 36g protein, 25.9g carbohydrate (of which 3.1g sugars), 46.1g fat (of which 17.4g saturates), 1.3g fibre and 0.19g salt.

  • Time: 50 mins
  • Serves: 3
Ingredients Quantity
short pasta,such as penne 160g/5¾oz
butter 25g/1oz
olive oil 1 tbsp
leek, thinly sliced 1 large
spring onions, thinly sliced 4
fresh thyme leaves 1 tbsp
salmon fillets, skin removed 2 x 120g/4¼oz
lemon, zest and juice 1
full-fat crème fraîche salt and freshly ground black pepper 3 tbsp

Method

Step 1:Cook the pasta in a saucepan of boiling water according to the packet instructions until just tender (al dente). Drain and reserve 100ml/3½fl oz cooking water

Step 2:Meanwhile, heat the butter and oil in a shallow casserole dish or frying pan with a lid over a medium–low heat. Fry the leek, spring onion and thyme leaves for 5 minutes, stirring regularly until starting to soften. Do not allow the leeks to take on any colour – keep an eye on them and reduce the temperature if necessary.

Step 3:Place the salmon fillets on top of the leeks, season really well with salt and pepper and add the lemon zest and juice. Cover with a lid and continue to cook for 5 minutes. Remove the lid, flip the salmon over, cover and cook for a further 3 minutes.

Step 4:Transfer the salmon to a plate and set aside. Stir the crème fraîche into the leek mixture then slowly add the reserved pasta water. Bring to a bubble and season to taste with salt and pepper.

Step 5:Stir in the pasta and allow to heat through. Divide between two plates and flake a salmon fillet over each plate.
